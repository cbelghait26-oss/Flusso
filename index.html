Problem

Most productivity apps isolate tasks from meaning.

When tasks are disconnected from objectives, prioritization becomes reactive and focus becomes fragile.

Lists grow. Clarity shrinks.

Without a higher objective, tasks compete for attention and urgency wins.

Motivation drops faster.

When the “why” is missing, persistence decreases and the system turns into noise.

Method

The Flusso method

Define objectives, link tasks, execute structured focus sessions, then reinforce alignment through feedback.

1) Objectives define direction

Set what you want to move forward (weekly, monthly, long-term).

2) Tasks link to objectives

Every task has context — improving prioritization and reducing friction.

3) Focus sessions execute

Timer/Pomodoro structure to reduce context switching and drift.

4) Measure what you do

Track focused time and completed actions to calibrate the system.

Psychology: Flusso’s task→objective linking matches a core idea from Goal Systems Theory (Kruglanski): when lower-level actions are connected to higher-order goals, people tend to persist longer and experience more meaning in execution.

Practical translation: tasks feel lighter when they clearly serve something bigger.
